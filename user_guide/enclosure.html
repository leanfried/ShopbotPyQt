The purpose of the UV enclosure is to protect the user from eye damage from the UV lamp. The enclosure can also protect the user and the environment around the Shopbot from splashes and sputters. For easiest assembly, the enclosure should be assembled around the Shopbot, after all of the printer parts have been installed.

Assembly instructions

0. Cut and drill all parts, according to machine shop diagrams.

1. Assemble the bottom frame with 2 L_depth and 2 L_width bars. L_depth bars should sit on top, inside of the L_depth bars. The feet feed through the holes at the ends of the L_depth and L_width bars. Hinge holes (triple holes), which won't be used, should sit on bottom of the assembly.
2. Add vertical corner bars, with 2 L_height_D and 2 L_height_L bars. These bars should sit inside the corners of the bottom frame. Make sure that the more closely spaced hinge holes are on the front and back (wide faces) of the enclosure.
3. Assemble the top frame with 1 L_depth, 1 L_depth_right, and 2 L_width bars. L_width bars should sit on top of the L_height bars, and L_depth bars should sit on top of the L_width bars. Hinge holes (triple holes) should sit on top of the assembly.
4. Add flat bars with 4 bar_height and 1 bar_height bars. Bar_height bars should go inside of the L-bars, and the bar_depth bar goes outside of the L-bars.
5. Add catches to the flat bars. These add a magnetic closure to the doors, so they don't drift open.
6. Assemble doors. Each door should have a handle, a strike (from the magnetic latches), and two hinges. It may be easier to assemble the latches directly on the frame instead of directly on the door. The washer goes between the hinge and the frame, and the screw goes on the other side of the frame. This will leave a small gap between the door and the frame, so it can swing without hitting other doors.
7. Attach doors to the frame. The small top door will leave a gap above the nozzle for wires and tubes.
